Your Change Compass: Navigating Uncertainty with Confidence

Change Readiness Job Aid

By Samantha Stockwell

1. Find Your Why

Change is challenging! In order to manage change successfully, you need to find your reason why. Sustained motivation and, ultimately, success is determined by your reason why. Ask yourself:

  • How will this change benefit me?
  • How might this change benefit my company, my community, or my customers?
  • What consequences would failing to change have?
  • Where does this change align with my personal and professional goals?
  • Where does this chagne align with my company's vision and goals?


    • 2. Check Your Readiness

    Knowing where you are now will help you prepare for change. Reflect honestly on what you are feeling and why without self-judgment. Ask yourself:

  • How am I feeling about this change? Nervous? Excited? Uncertain? Angry? Take inventory of all your emotions related to the change.
  • What are my challenges when it comes to this change? Examine your potential barries like workload, current skills, and habits.
  • What are my strenghts when it comes to this change? Recognize the skills, attitudes, and experiences you have that can help you succeed.


    • 3. Stay Informed and Engaged

    Denial is a normal part of the change journey, but staying in that phase can get it the way of your success. Engaging with the change process and keeping yourself informed, even if the change is making you nervous, will set you up for better outcomes and may even ease your anxiety over time. Try:

  • Make time for any available training and resources.
  • Ask questions and share your thoughts.
  • Contribute to solutions or even seek opportunities to be part of pilot groups.


    • 4. Build Your Skills and Confidence

    A chief driver of chnage resistance is fear that you won't be able to succeed in the future state. Building your skills and feeding your confidence will counteract that fear and remind you that you can succeed! Try:

  • Partner up with a peer or mentor and work to learn together.
  • Take advantage of opportunities for training and practice.
  • Check in with yourself and your change partner regularly. What have you accomplished? What do you need to adjust?
  • Break down big processes into manageable steps. What's one small thing you can do to build your skills?
  • Remember that every skill you build will serve you in the future.
  • Celebrate your wins. Each small win will build your confidence and remind you that you have what it takes to thrive through change.


    • 5. Take Care of Yourself

    Change is often stressful. Taking care of yourself throughout the process is important. Try:

  • Acknowledge your feelings--even the ones you aren't proud of. Fear, stress, and even grief for the way things were are normal during times of change. Give yourself space to feel these things without judgment.
  • Get support. Talk to a trusted friend, use your company Employee Assistance Program, or, better yet, both. Having a sounding board to talk through your experiences with will help you process.
  • Engage in wellness and relience practices like rest, movement, mindfulness, and balance outside of work.
